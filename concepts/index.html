Concepts

Understanding the difference between privacy, security, and anonymity is essential for practicing operational security (OPSEC).

Core Concepts

Privacy vs. Anonymity

  • Privacy: Control over your data and who sees it (e.g., "I encrypt my messages so only the recipient can read them").
  • Anonymity: Concealing your identity entirely (e.g., "I browse this forum, but nobody knows who I actually am in the physical world").

Metadata

Even if your message contents are encrypted, metadata (who you talk to, when, from where, and for how long) can still leak your identity. True anonymity requires metadata minimization.

Threat Modeling

A threat model is a process by which you identify potential security threats and define countermeasures.

  • Who are you hiding from?
  • What assets are you protecting?
  • What are the consequences of a leak?
