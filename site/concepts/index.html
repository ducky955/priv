    Core Concepts

    Understanding the difference between privacy, security, and anonymity is essential for practicing operational security (OPSEC).

    Privacy vs. Security vs. Anonymity

    • Privacy: Control over your data and who sees it
    • Security: Protection of your systems and data from unauthorized access
    • Anonymity: Concealing your identity entirely

    Key Topics

    Metadata

    Even if your message contents are encrypted, metadata (who you talk to, when, from where, and for how long) can still leak your identity. True anonymity requires metadata minimization.

    Threat Modeling

    A threat model is a process by which you identify potential security threats and define countermeasures: - Who are you hiding from? - What assets are you protecting? - What are the consequences of a leak?

    Encryption

    Understand different types of encryption and when they're appropriate: - Symmetric encryption - Asymmetric encryption - End-to-end encryption
