# Concepts Overview

## Fundamental Principles

### Privacy vs. Anonymity

**Privacy** means controlling what information about yourself is visible to others:
- Example: Using encrypted messaging so only the recipient can read your messages
- Focus: What information is disclosed

**Anonymity** means concealing your identity entirely:
- Example: Browsing a forum without anyone knowing who you actually are
- Focus: Who you are

### Metadata and Its Importance

Metadata is information *about* your communications rather than the communications themselves:

- **Who**: Identifies of communication parties
- **When**: Timing of communications
- **Where**: Location and source/destination addresses
- **How**: Duration and type of communication

Even with perfect encryption, metadata leaks can reveal sensitive information about your activities and relationships.

### Threat Modeling Process

Effective protection starts with understanding your specific threats:

1. **Identify your adversary**: Who specifically are you protecting against?
2. **Define your assets**: What needs protection?
3. **Assess consequences**: What happens if something is compromised?
4. **Choose countermeasures**: Select tools and practices matching your threat level
