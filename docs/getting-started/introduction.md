# Introduction to Online Anonymity

This introductory guide covers the fundamentals of online anonymity and why it matters in today's digital world.

## Key Concepts

- **Privacy vs. Anonymity**: Privacy controls *what* information is visible. Anonymity conceals *who* you are.
- **Metadata**: Information about your communications (who, when, where) can leak your identity even if message contents are encrypted.
- **Threat Model**: Understanding *who* you're hiding from and *what* you're protecting helps you choose appropriate tools and practices.

## Why Anonymity Matters

In an era of widespread surveillance, data breaches, and digital tracking, online anonymity provides:

- Protection from targeted attacks
- Freedom of expression without fear of retaliation
- Privacy from corporate data collection
- Security against state-level surveillance
